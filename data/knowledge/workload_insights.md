# Workload and Learning Correlation (SYNTHETIC)

> Source ID: KB-WORKLOAD-003 — approved internal workload study (fabricated for demo).

## Insights
- Employees with **more than 20 meeting hours per week** show lower study completion.
- Optimal completion appears when learners have **12–18 meeting hours** and **at least
  15 focus hours** per week.
- A realistic weekly study budget is roughly **40% of available focus hours**, reduced
  further when meeting load is high.

## Sustainability / Burnout Signals
- Sustained study load above **~90% of available weekly capacity for 6+ weeks** is a
  burnout risk and tends to *reduce* retention rather than improve it.
- Repeated **after-hours study sessions** and rising meeting load are early warning
  signs of unsustainable load.
- A fatigue signature is **more hours studied with flat or falling practice scores**.

## Recommendation
Schedule learning blocks during focus-heavy periods and inside the learner's preferred
slot. Do not push a plan that exceeds sustainable weekly capacity; extend the timeline
or redistribute instead.
